GulabJamun

Description

Ingredients

  1. Main Ingredients for Dough:
    • 1 cup milk powder (full-cream or skimmed)
    • ¼ to ½ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    • ¼ to ½ tsp baking powder
    • 1–2 tbsp ghee or melted butter
    • 4–5 tbsp whole milk or water
    • ½ tsp cardamom powder
    • 1–2 tbsp lemon juice (optional, to prevent syrup crystallization)
    • Oil or ghee (for deep frying)
  2. For Sugar Syrup:
    • 1½ to 2 cups sugar
    • ¾ to 1 cup water
    • 4–6 green cardamom pods, crushed
    • 1–2 tsp rose water or rose essence
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
    • 1–2 tbsp lemon juice (optional)

Steps to follow:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix milk powder, flour, baking powder, and cardamom
    • Rub in ghee until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Add milk and lemon juice gradually, mixing with fingertips to form a soft, slightly sticky dough.
    • Do not overwork—the dough should be pliable but not wet.
  2. Rest the Dough:
    • Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15–30 minutes.
    • This helps the flour and milk powder hydrate fully, ensuring soft, juicy gulab jamuns.
  3. Make the Syrup:
    • In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, cardamom pods, and lemon juice.
    • Bring to a boil, stirring until sugar dissolves.
    • Simmer for 5–7 minutes until the syrup becomes slightly thick.
    • Remove from heat, add rose water and saffron. Let cool slightly.
  4. Shape the Balls:
    • Divide the dough into 15–20g portions.
    • Roll each into a smooth, crack-free ball using oiled hands.
    • Ensure no folds or cracks—this prevents splitting during frying.
  5. Fry the Gulab Jamuns:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over medium-low heat (around 130–150°C).
    • Test by dropping a small dough piece—it should float and turn golden slowly.
    • Fry in batches, gently rotating with a slotted spoon for even browning.
    • Cook for 5–7 minutes until golden brown and puffed.
  6. Soak in Syrup:
    • Drain fried gulab jamuns on paper towels. Immediately add them to the warm syrup.
    • Let them soak for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight, for maximum flavor and softness.
  7. Serve:
    • Serve warm or at room temperature.
    • Garnish with chopped pistachios or saffron strands.
    • For extra indulgence, serve with ice cream.
